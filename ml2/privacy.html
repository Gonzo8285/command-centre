Privacy notice

Last updated 2026-05-18.

ML² Consulting Ltd ("ML²", "we") is the data controller for the personal data we collect through this website. This notice explains what we collect, why, how long we keep it, and your rights under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

What we collect

  • Information you give us — name, email, phone, and message content when you contact us via the email link or phone number on this site.
  • Browser-side flag — a single value (ml2_seen_notice) stored in your browser's localStorage so the cookie notice doesn't reappear. We never transmit this value off your device.

We do not use tracking cookies, advertising pixels, third-party analytics, or any form of behavioural tracking on this site. The only third-party request is to the Inter font CDN at rsms.me, which receives standard HTTP headers from your browser; we don't control what they log.

Why we hold contact data

  • To respond to your enquiry (lawful basis: legitimate interests).
  • To follow up about a proposal or engagement (lawful basis: legitimate interests or, where applicable, contract).
  • If we move to a contract, to deliver the engagement and meet our legal/accounting obligations (lawful basis: contract, legal obligation).

How long we keep it

  • Enquiries that don't lead to a contract — deleted after 12 months.
  • Contracted engagements — retained for 7 years after the engagement ends to satisfy accounting and tax record requirements.

Who else sees it

Your contact data stays with ML². We use Microsoft 365 (Outlook) for email, and our standard professional advisors (accountant, etc.) for limited financial / legal purposes. We don't sell, rent, or share your data with marketers.

Your rights

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction — ask us to fix anything inaccurate.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data (subject to legal retention obligations).
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured format.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Complaint — to the ICO (ico.org.uk) if you think we've handled your data unlawfully.
